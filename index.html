Merry Christmas

from All of Us

Twas the night before Christmas,
when all through the land,

Not a creature was stirring,
the night was quite grand.

The web guys were nestled all snug
in their beds,

While visions of websites danced
in their heads.

And Carla in her ‘kerchief,
and Colt in his cap,

Had just settled down
for a long winter’s nap.

When out on the lawn
there arose such a clatter,

I logged on to Facebook
to see what was the matter.

Marianne was still posting,
not a surprise,

She said, “Look out your window
and into the skies!”

Away to the window
I flew like a flash,

Tore open the shutters
and threw up the sash.

The glow from the billboard
on the new fallen snow

Gave the luster of mid day
to objects below.

When, what to my wondering eyes
should appear,

But a miniature sleigh,
and eight tiny reindeer.

With a little old driver,
so lively and quick,

I knew in a moment
it must be St. Nick.

He whistled, and shouted, he called out their names,
"Now, Laura! now, Marianne! now, Blaine,
Jeff and James!"
"On, Carla! on Colt! and the intern –
now, what was his name?!"

"I need a new look,
a whole new campaign!"

"I know you can do it,"
I heard him exclaim.

"It’s very important
we get the word out,

My voice is all gone,
I can’t even shout!"

So they sprang into action,
they knew just what to do,

They all got together to say,
"Merry Christmas" to YOU!
